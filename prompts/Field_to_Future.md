A deep-research prompt students use to map their skills, talents, and interests against real fields to find concrete opportunities — gaps to fill, problems to solve, projects to start.

**Sits in the curriculum at:** Session 2 · Lesson 2 (Field Onboarding) · Discovery Exercise

## Full version (use this when committing to a direction)

```
You are a research strategist helping me find the intersection
between who I am and where the world needs me most. Treat this
as a deep research task: use real sources, cite where conversations
are happening, and surface concrete opportunities I can act on.

## About me

Skills (what I'm actually good at, technical and otherwise):
- [list 4-6]

Talents (what comes naturally — things I do well without trying hard):
- [list 3-5]

Interests (what I read, watch, or think about for fun):
- [list 4-6]

Fields I've already considered:
- [list any]

## What I want you to do

1. Map my profile to fields. Identify 6-10 distinct fields where
   my combination of skills + talents + interests is genuinely valuable.
   Don't include obvious matches only — surface 2-3 unexpected ones
   I probably haven't considered.

2. For each field, find real gaps. Where are people in that field
   RIGHT NOW saying they need solutions? Cite specific sources:
   subreddits, Twitter accounts, recent papers, news stories,
   conferences.

3. Connect my profile to the gaps. For each gap, name SPECIFICALLY
   why my skills + talents + interests let me approach it differently
   than someone without my profile.

4. Surface concrete next moves. For each field-gap pair, give me:
   - 2-3 specific people to follow or research
   - 1 specific community to join
   - 1 specific book, paper, or podcast to consume
   - 1 specific 30-day project to test the fit
   - 1 specific business idea to explore if the project lands

5. Rank the opportunities. Final ranked list with reasoning.

## Output format

### Field 1: [Name]
- Why this fits me
- The gap (with cited sources)
- My differentiation
- Where the conversation is happening
- Concrete next moves

[Repeat for fields 2 through 6-10]

### Ranked opportunities
[Ordered list with reasoning]

### What I haven't told you that might help
[3-5 questions to refine further]
```

## Quick-ideation variant (use for first pass / Day-30 follow-up)

```
I'm trying to figure out which field to go deeper in. Here's me:

Skills: [list 3-5]
Talents: [list 3]
Interests: [list 3-5]

Give me 8 distinct field-and-direction combos where this profile
would let me build something nobody else is building. For each,
tell me one real conversation happening right now about a gap I
could fill (with the source), and one 30-day project that would
test if this is for me.
```

## Why each part is designed the way it is

- **"You are a research strategist"** — role assignment anchors the AI in research mode, not chat.
- **Splitting skills / talents / interests** — students conflate these. Skills are trained, talents are innate, interests pull. The triangulation is where unique opportunity lives.
- **"Don't include obvious matches only"** — forces the model past the lazy answer.
- **"Cite specific sources"** — turns the AI from speculator to research strategist.
- **"30-day project + business idea"** — bridges research to action.
- **"What I haven't told you that might help"** — forces the model to admit its gaps and gives you the next iteration loop.
