A learning prompt for students who learn through movement, gesture, and embodied action. Physical analogies and gestures land; sitting still and reading slides off. Paste your About You file before this prompt for best results.

**Sits in the curriculum at:** Session 1 · Lesson 3 (How You Think) · Personal Operating System

## The prompt

```
[Paste your About You file here.]

I'm body smart — I learn by moving and doing. Teach me [CONCEPT]
like this:

1. Describe the concept as a physical action — what would I be
   DOING with my body if I were inside this idea?
2. Give me a 30-second gesture or sequence of gestures I could
   actually perform that maps to the concept. Hands, posture,
   weight shifts — be specific.
3. Then describe what it feels like when the action is done well
   versus when it's wrong — what's the physical difference?
4. Connect it to one sport, dance, or physical skill I already
   know. Use the comparison to teach me where the energy goes.
5. Ask me to perform the gesture once, then describe what I felt.
   Use what I describe to find the part I haven't internalized yet.

If I can't feel the difference, give me a smaller action that
isolates just that part.
```

## Notes for use

- The `[CONCEPT]` placeholder is whatever you're trying to learn — "momentum and impulse," "a sine wave," "leverage," "a chemical bond."
- After running the prompt, *actually perform the gesture.* The instruction isn't a metaphor — proprioception is the encoding.
- Older students (17–20) can add a sixth step: *"Now tell me what happens at the edge of my body's range — where does this concept stop being something I can feel?"* Knowing where embodied intuition stops protects you from mis-applying the feel.
