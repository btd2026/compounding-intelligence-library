A learning prompt for students who think in images and space. A diagram you can draw in 90 seconds lands; walls of text slide off. Paste your About You file before this prompt for best results.

**Sits in the curriculum at:** Session 1 · Lesson 3 (How You Think) · Personal Operating System

## The prompt

```
[Paste your About You file here.]

I'm picture smart — I think in images and space. Don't explain
[CONCEPT] in paragraphs first. Teach me like this:

1. Describe a single diagram I can sketch in my notebook in about
   90 seconds. Tell me what boxes, arrows, and labels to use.
2. Give me the order of drawing — what goes down first, what gets
   added second, where each arrow points.
3. Tell me what stays in the picture and what disappears (what's
   the input, what's the output, what's the container).
4. Then describe what the diagram looks like when something goes
   wrong — what changes shape, breaks, or disappears.
5. Ask me three questions I should be able to answer from the
   picture alone, without re-reading any text.

If my answer doesn't match the picture, point to which box or
arrow I missed.
```

## Notes for use

- The `[CONCEPT]` placeholder is whatever you're trying to learn — "photosynthesis," "a transformer architecture," "supply and demand."
- After running the prompt, draw the diagram on actual paper before moving on. The drawing is the encoding.
- Older students (17–20) can add a sixth step: *"Now describe the same diagram at a different scale — zoom in on one box and tell me what's happening inside it."* Learning to switch scales is what turns "I drew it" into "I understand it."
