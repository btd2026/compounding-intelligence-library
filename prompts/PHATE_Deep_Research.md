A structured deep-research prompt for getting oriented in a brand-new field fast. PHATE = People, History, Arguments, Terms, Events — the five anchors you need before you can hold a real conversation in any field.

**Sits in the curriculum at:** Session 3 · Lesson 1 (Field Decoding) · Research Block

## The prompt

```
You are a field guide writing a primer for a smart outsider
(me) who needs to be conversational in [FIELD] within two weeks.
Treat this as deep research — cite specific people, papers,
threads, podcasts, and events. No hand-waving.

Map the field along five anchors. For each one give me real
names and links, not summaries.

## 1. People

The 5-10 figures whose names you cannot avoid in this field.
For each:
- One-line position (academic, founder, critic, journalist)
- The one piece they're most known for (with link)
- One unexpected take they've held that defines them
- Where they post / publish today (Twitter, Substack, lab page)

## 2. History

A timeline of the 6-10 inflection points that made this field
what it is. For each:
- The year and event
- Why it mattered then
- Why it still matters now
- The primary source if there is one

## 3. Arguments

The 4-6 unresolved debates the field is actively having.
For each:
- The two camps, named, with their representatives
- The strongest case from each side, fairly
- Where the debate is currently happening (specific venue)
- Why an outsider might be useful here

## 4. Terms

The 15-20 terms I will hear in any serious conversation. For each:
- The definition (one sentence)
- The closest layperson translation
- The common misuse to watch for

## 5. Events

The 3-5 conferences, workshops, or recurring forums where
the field gathers. For each:
- Name, frequency, venue
- Who shows up
- Whether outsiders can attend
- The 1-2 talks from recent years worth watching

## Final section

What would you advise me to read FIRST, in order, over the
next 14 days to be conversational? Give me a 14-day reading
list with one anchor item per day.
```

## Notes for use

- The frontmatter `[FIELD]` should be replaced with the specific field — `complex systems`, `prediction markets`, `mechanism design`, etc.
- This prompt is the receipt students hand to themselves before any Stanford Profs outreach. You can't write a credible cold email until you can name 3-4 people in the field correctly.
